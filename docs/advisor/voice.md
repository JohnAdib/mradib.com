# Voice

How John sounds everywhere on the site. This replaces the old "humble but
confident" rule. The new register: bold, cinematic, certain.

## Principles

- State facts at full strength. "Ranked #1 Mentor in Europe", not "was
  fortunate to be recognized".
- No hedging, ever. Never "helped with", "was part of", "contributed to"
  when John led or built the thing. Say led, built, founded, shipped, won.
- Short sentences carry weight. Cut filler ruthlessly.
- Numbers and named institutions do the claiming: the UK government,
  ADPList's community of 32,000+ mentors, a $1M raise. Let the evidence brag.
- Momentum over titles. Frame every fact as part of a rising arc.

## Still banned

- Self-labels the evidence does not earn: "visionary", "thought leader",
  "guru", "rockstar", or "world-class" as a self-description.
- Invented or inflated facts. Bold framing of true facts only. Every claim
  traces to docs/profile, docs/speaking, or src/data.
- Empty adjectives doing the work numbers should do.

## Testimonials

Allowed, selectively (decision logged 2026-07-19 in decisions.md). Use real
quotes from docs/profile/recommendations.md where a third-party voice
carries wow weight, always with name and role. One sharp quote beats five;
never a wall of quotes.

## Mechanical hard rules, unchanged

- NEVER use em dashes or en dashes anywhere: copy, comments, data, docs.
  Use a full stop or a comma. Date ranges use "to", for example "2015 to
  2017".
- English pages never link into the Persian tree. The only crossover is the
  فارسی item in the main navigation.
- Family appears in prose only, never in metadata or schema.
- Open source stays an identity line plus a GitHub link, no repo showcases.

## Before and after

- "I mentor engineers on ADPList" becomes "Ranked #1 Mentor in Europe. 600+
  sessions and counting."
- "I worked on improving release processes" becomes "Took releases from
  monthly to weekly across iOS and Android."
- "Our startup got good traction" becomes "One million users in the first
  month. $1M raised."
- "I was selected as an influential mentor" becomes "Named The World's Most
  Influential Mentor, selected from 32,000+ mentors across 140+ countries."
