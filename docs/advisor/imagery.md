# Imagery

The advisor picks images; John should not have to. When he asks "which
photo", apply this hierarchy, state the pick and why, and log notable picks
in decisions.md.

## Selection hierarchy

1. Stage shots: John speaking, audience visible, motion and scale.
2. Candid leadership moments: workshops, panels, mentoring in action.
3. Award and ceremony shots: medals, certificates being received.
4. Strong portraits: confident, well lit, looking at or past the camera.
5. Last resort: desk shots, screenshots, logos.

Prefer images that contain other people, scale, or motion. A room full of
people listening to John says "leader" louder than any headline.

## Per-page guidance

- Homepage hero: the single strongest image on the site. A stage shot or a
  portrait with presence. Never a casual snapshot.
- About chapters: one image per wow beat where available (WorldSkills medal,
  startup era, London stages). Images advance the story.
- Talk pages: stage or venue shot first; a slides screenshot only as
  fallback.
- Award pages: evidence imagery (certificate, ceremony, badge) at full
  native resolution, existing convention.
- OG images: see playbooks/seo.md. Every page ships one at 1200x630.

## Quality bar

- Full native resolution, no upscaled or blurry sources.
- Consistent treatment within a page type: same aspect ratios, same tone.
- If no image meets the bar, ask John for one and say exactly what shot is
  needed, for example "a wide stage shot from the JavaScript London talk".
  Never ship a weak image silently.
