# Playbook: homepage

The most valuable page on the site. The primary reader, a founder or
investor who searched John's name, gives it 5 seconds.

## Philosophy

Curate, do not enumerate. The homepage introduces the arc, shows a few
undeniable proofs, and sends visitors to the hub pages (/awards, /talks,
/articles). Full lists live on the hubs.

## The 5-second test

Above the fold, the reader must get: who John is (builder and leader), one
or two proofs they cannot argue with, and a reason to scroll. If the first
screen could describe a thousand engineers, it fails.

## Hierarchy of proof

Order highlights by wow weight for the primary audience:

1. The World's Most Influential Mentor, selected from 32,000+ mentors across
   140+ countries.
2. 2x co-founder: $1M raised, one million users in a month, 1,200+
   businesses served.
3. UK government endorsement: Global Talent, exceptional talent in
   technology.
4. Stages: AI Coding Summit (5,000+ engineers), React Advanced London at
   Figma.
5. AI-first practice at Zapp: releases taken from monthly to weekly, AI
   reviewers on every pull request.

## Rules

- A stat earns its place only if it means something on its own to someone
  who has never heard of ADPList or Zapp. Add context ("from 32,000+
  mentors") or cut it.
- Every section ends with a path deeper: About, a talk page, an award page.
- Never add a homepage section without removing or tightening another. The
  page gets sharper, not longer.
