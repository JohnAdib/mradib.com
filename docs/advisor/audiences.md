# Audiences, ranked

Every framing, SEO, and image decision optimizes for the primary audience
first. Serve the rest only when it costs the primary nothing.

## 1. Founders, investors, and people more successful than John (primary)

They heard the name, searched it, and landed here. They give the site 5
seconds. In those 5 seconds they must feel: this guy is different, nothing
is impossible for him, what a story. They respect evidence of scale ($1M
raised, one million users, 5,000+ audiences), institutional recognition (UK
government, ADPList100), and visible momentum. They are allergic to CV
language and hedging.

## 2. Conference organizers

Need instant speaker credibility: stages already played, audience sizes,
recordings one click away. Served mainly by /talks and the talk pages.

## 3. Senior hiring managers and executives

Need leadership evidence and impact numbers. Served by the same leader
framing; no page is ever written "for recruiters".

## 4. Mentees and engineers

Need approachability and a way in. Served by /mentor and the guides. Warmth
lives there; it never dilutes the leader frame on the homepage or About.

## The 5-second rule per page

- Homepage: the primary reader must see the arc and one or two undeniable
  proofs before scrolling.
- About: must open with a hook that promises a story, not a bio.
- Talk pages: stage, audience size, recording. Proof before content.
- Awards: issuer prestige first (government, global community), then the
  list.
